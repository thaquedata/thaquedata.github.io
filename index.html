About Me

I am an Astrophysicist and programming instructor with a demonstrated history of working in the higher education industry. I incorporate physics, mathematics and scientific algorithm together to inspire my students solve complex programming challenges. I currently work as a coding instrutcor and mentor at Coding With Kids.

I am skilled in teaching physical sciences, problem-solving with Python, teaching Web Development with CSS, HTML, and Javascript, and creating Game Development contents in python. By the way, check out some of my awesome projects that I built for my students.

Work

I uphold an effective communication skill and adaptability with teaching strategies to deliver excellent education in group and private online environments. Currently I am holding a leadership position in our company to work with incoming new instructors. I offer support and feedback before and during new instructors' first lesson, along with ongoing assistance, as needed, throughout their journey to being promoted from Instructor in Training to full and successful instructors.

I am a strong research professional with a Master's degree focused in Astrophysics from the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth. Teaching Philosophy

I grew up in a liberal, education-orineted environment that always promoted personal expression. My approach to teaching reflects this concept thoroughly. I value my students as whole human beings with valid feelings, preferences and learning curves.

Throughout my teaching career, I have taught in multiple levels, from middle school coding to freshmen calculus-based courses in college. The pre-calculus skills I teach in my tenth grade web development courses are important, surely, but they are secondary to the life skills I try to instill in them. Consequently, my responsibilities must include steering them in the direction of life-enriching, identity-shaping activities.

I use ten minutes at the end of each class for open discussion, often prompted by current events or a hypothetical question I pose to students. These lively forums are my favorite part of my day. After eight years of teaching, I am still surprised nearly daily with the perspectives of my students.

During the class period, I implement applicable illustrations of concepts and encourage students to use their interests and curiosity to develop their own real-life examples. I use those examples and encourage them to write coding scripts to translate the problems into scientific algorithms. As often as possible, I incorporate individual expression and creativity into game development projects, where they can design their own rules.

Even within a limited scope of educational subject matter, I believe that my job is to teach students more than Science and Programming. My role is to help empower students to own their identities, express their individuality and gain independence.
